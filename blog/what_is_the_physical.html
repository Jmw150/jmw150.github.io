What Is The Physical

I think physicality may be similar to syllables. The distinction between what is and is not physical may be a thing that human beings naturally intuit. And it is a definition that humans adhere to for similar functional reasons. But with the modern need for clarity and precision, physics may not be able to hide in the human subconscious as a meaningful category.

Also, side note, and to cut off the obvious escape into a seemly larger category, metaphysics is a stacked tuple that came with a name based on its order in a book series, not some property relating to modern physics. It turns out metaphysics proper is: 'first philosophy’, ‘first science’, ‘wisdom’, and ‘theology’. Not a study devoted to matters that transcend the mundane concerns of Newton and Einstein and Heisenberg. With so many first-* statements, it would make metaphysics seem meta. But Aristotle, if I remember the readings of those books, goes on about the basics of things, and ends with deep understanding and belief. So, alas, no superset of concerns along this route.

Back to the question of what is physical, another classic trap is relying on what is called sense data. This is where odd questions like "is math real?" come from. I do not think such arguments survived to the modern age though. [1] What seems to work for now is abstract structures. Given evidence of some kind for such a structure is then sufficient to demonstrate it can be relied upon for daily work.
