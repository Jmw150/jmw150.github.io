Write More Notes

I like thinking and talking. I think my insights should be shared, without cost. I do not meet very many people that think exactly like I do. Although that may just be a lack of social skills to find such people.

There are millions of researchers across the world. Generic information is basically free. And I may not have anything really novel to add. But it is good practice for me to try to form my thoughts, instead of just being some sort of sponge for arcane knowledge and producer of programs. And even though there is such a deluge of information, I say it is not at max capacity. A decade or two ago there were not memes about advanced mathematics. There is no way to track all of it, but I would say our shared knowledge about everything is getting deeper. Not to mention there are algorithms to sort through it all now.

Some people just write a lot. Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz wrote enough to fill a library room. Only a few of his ideas are read by many, but they really enriched society by being written. Good professors seem to have something new to say each year.

An academic should write more notes as they go through life experiences.

I think I have a decent memory for things that I like. It was enough to get through primary school and undergrad without taking any notes. I can just pay attention in class and remember enough to get an A. To help soak up more for a tough class, I would read the book first. Taking brief notes in class is probably best. But it is good to take those notes and expand on them after the class.

Graduate school it is different. For one, the space of knowledge a person is expected to work with is the entire modern field of whatever is being studied. The second, a person in graduate school is expected to teach this stuff for some reason. That reason, is why notes are important to take.

I have, for years, tutored people in math and computer things. I tend to talk at my level of understanding though. I have the patience to slow down if that does not work. And I can usually be patient until the information clicks.

But teaching a class, or giving a seminar, are sadly different. I have to talk in the absence of receiving communication for about an hour. And what is said has to make sense on the first pass for as many people listening as possible. This can be done organically. But there is a stark contrast for most people between a planned speech and an impromptu one. Taking notes is a simple way to analyse and improve one's own knowledge and delivery.
